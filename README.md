# Gratitude Journal

## Overview

A reflective gratitude journal capturing the process of learning, building, and staying grounded while developing a purpose-driven public safety platform.

---

## Entry 1 — Learning Something New

Today I’m grateful for pushing through something that initially felt unfamiliar. Setting up a development environment and beginning to build an application from scratch is something I had not done before. Even when I did not fully understand each step, I stayed with it and kept moving forward.

This experience reminded me that I don’t need to have everything figured out at once. I just need to stay engaged and continue learning.

---

## Entry 2 — Access to Tools and Knowledge

I’m grateful to have access to tools that make it possible to move from idea to execution. Being able to use AI, coding platforms, and structured learning environments has changed what feels possible.

This access allows me to think bigger and act on ideas that would have previously felt out of reach.

---

## Entry 3 — Purpose Behind the Work

I’m grateful that the work I am doing has meaning. The project I am building is connected to real issues involving missing women, children, and underserved communities.

That gives the work weight. It is not just technical—it is purposeful.

---

## Entry 4 — Growth Mindset

I’m grateful for recognizing that discomfort is part of the process. There were moments where I didn’t understand what was happening or why something wasn’t working.

Instead of stopping, I kept going and asked questions. That shift in mindset is something I value.

---

## Entry 5 — Small Wins

I’m grateful for small wins today:

- installing Node successfully  
- running commands in the terminal  
- creating my first app environment  
- setting up my GitHub repository  

Each step builds momentum. Progress is happening, even when it feels slow.

---

## Reflection

This process is teaching me that growth comes from consistency. I’m learning to approach new challenges with curiosity instead of hesitation, and to stay grounded while building something meaningful.
